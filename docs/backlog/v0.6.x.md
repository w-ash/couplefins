### v0.6.0: Settlement Recording
**Goal**: Record payments between the couple to close out a month's balance.
**Context**: The reconciliation engine computes "Alice owes Bob $147.50" but there's no way to record that the payment actually happened. The couple settles up via Venmo/Zelle/cash, then has no record in the app.
**What this unlocks**: Completing the core loop â€” upload â†’ reconcile â†’ settle â†’ finalize becomes a tracked end-to-end workflow.
**Key tech choices**: `Settlement` entity with payment method enum. Recording a payment is not guarded by finalization (it's documenting what happened), but deleting one is. Settlement amount pre-filled from computed balance.

#### Settlement Domain + Persistence

- [ ] **Settlement entity + repository**
    - Effort: S
    - Dependencies: v0.5.1
    - What: New `Settlement` frozen attrs entity: id, year, month, amount, from_person_id, to_person_id, method (Venmo/Zelle/Cash/Other), settled_at, notes. New `SettlementRepositoryProtocol` with `save()`, `get_by_period()`, `get_by_year()`, `delete()`. Alembic migration for `settlements` table. Wire into `UnitOfWorkProtocol`.
    - Why: Persistence foundation for the settlement workflow

#### Settlement Use Cases

- [ ] **RecordSettlement + GetSettlements + DeleteSettlement use cases**
    - Effort: M
    - Dependencies: "Settlement entity + repository"
    - What: `RecordSettlementUseCase` creates a settlement record (validates period exists, amount positive, person IDs exist). `GetSettlementsUseCase` fetches settlements for a period. `DeleteSettlementUseCase` removes a mistaken entry (guarded by finalization).
    - Why: Core backend logic for settlement tracking
    - Notes:
        - Recording is not finalization-guarded â€” paying a debt is documenting reality
        - Deletion is finalization-guarded â€” can't un-record a payment on a locked month
